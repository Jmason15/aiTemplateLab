The AIC Accident Incident Claims (AIC) system is a comprehensive claims management platform designed to streamline the reporting, processing, and resolution of both citizen-reported incidents and employee-related accidents involving state property and personnel. Key Program Features Dual Reporting Sides: Citizen Side (Incident): Citizens report incidents—such as property damage or personal injury—through a structured online workflow. Employee Side (Accident): Employees either respond to citizen-reported incidents or independently report accidents involving state property or equipment. Citizen Incident Notification Workflow: Citizens complete a detailed form that guides them through sections including personal and incident details, attachments, affected vehicle and property information, injury specifics, and police report documentation. After submission, citizens receive confirmation and a PDF copy of their claim for their records. Internal Processing – HROS & Employee Involvement: The Human Resources Occupational Safety (HROS) team reviews citizen submissions, then prompts a relevant employee to file an associated accident report on the same case. This ensures all details are corroborated and claims are thoroughly documented for both internal and external requirements. Reporting Pathways For Citizens: They submit a single type of report: "Citizen Incident Notification." For Employees: Employees can file claims based on: "Citizen Reported": Formally responding to a citizen's original report as assigned by HROS. "MDT Employee": Reporting state property damage without a related citizen report. Personal Vehicle Accidents: Employees use the citizen reporting mechanism if their own vehicle is involved. System Objectives Trend Analysis & Risk Management: The AIC program centralizes and digitizes all reports to track incident and accident trends, facilitating proactive risk management. Automated Documentation: The system generates claim packages for the Risk Management Tort Defense division, ensuring compliance and a full audit trail. Process Consistency: Both sides follow similar workflows with adjustments for the nature of the reporter (citizen or employee), standardizing data collection, review, and notification procedures. This system streamlines every aspect of the claims lifecycle—from first notice to claim package submission—guaranteeing efficient communication, regulatory compliance, and effective trend monitoring for improved operational safety and accountability.
